Satay is a popular Southeast Asian dish consisting of skewered and grilled meat, typically served with a rich and creamy peanut sauce. The meat can vary from chicken to beef or lamb, and itâ€™s often accompanied by rice cakes or a side of cucumber salad.

Ingredients

  • Chicken, beef, or lamb (cut into small cubes)
  • Satay marinade (typically contains soy sauce, garlic, turmeric, and coriander)
  • Skewers (wooden or metal)
  • Peanut butter
  • Coconut milk
  • Sugar
  • Chili paste or sambal (optional for heat)
  • Lime juice
  • Vegetable oil
  • Rice cakes (optional, for serving)
  • Cucumber (optional, for serving)

Steps

  1. Cut the chicken, beef, or lamb into small cubes and marinate them in a mixture of soy sauce, garlic, turmeric, and coriander for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Thread the marinated meat onto skewers, making sure the pieces are evenly spaced.
  3. Grill the skewers on medium heat, turning occasionally, until the meat is cooked through and slightly charred (about 5-7 minutes per side, depending on the meat).
  4. While the meat is grilling, prepare the peanut sauce by combining peanut butter, coconut milk, sugar, chili paste (if using), and lime juice in a saucepan. Heat over low heat until smooth and well combined.
  5. Serve the grilled satay with the peanut sauce on the side, accompanied by rice cakes and cucumber slices for a refreshing touch.
